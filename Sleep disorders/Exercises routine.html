Myofunctional Therapy Routine

Morning Routine (Approx. 10 minutes):

  1. Tongue Retraction (2 minutes)

    • Sit or stand comfortably.
    • Extend your tongue as far as possible.
    • Slowly retract your tongue back into your mouth.
    • Repeat this motion for 1-2 minutes.

  2. Jaw Opening and Closing (2 minutes)

    • Keep your mouth relaxed.
    • Gently open your jaw as wide as comfortable.
    • Slowly close your jaw.
    • Repeat this motion for 1-2 minutes.

Midday Routine (Approx. 10 minutes):

  1. Buteyko Breathing Method with Trainer (5 minutes)

    • Use the Buteyko Breathing Method Trainer.
    • Focus on controlled and shallow breathing.
    • Inhale gently through your nose for a count of 2.
    • Exhale slowly and completely through your nose for a count of 4.
    • Repeat this cycle for 5 minutes.

Evening Routine (Approx. 30 minutes to 1 hour):

  1. RespiBelt Respiratory Muscle Training (20-30 minutes)

    • Wear the RespiBelt during your evening exercises.
    • Perform a combination of deep breathing exercises and resistance training.
    • Follow these steps:
      1. Inhale deeply through your nose, expanding your diaphragm.
      2. Exhale slowly and completely through your nose, engaging your intercostal muscles.
      3. Repeat this deep breathing pattern for 10-15 minutes.
      4. Then, engage in resistance training by inhaling against the resistance provided by the RespiBelt for 5-10 minutes.

Before Bed Routine (Approx. 10 minutes):

  1. Gentle Jaw Stretching (2 minutes)

    • Stand or sit comfortably.
    • Gently open your mouth and stretch your jaw downward.
    • Hold the stretch for 10-15 seconds.
    • Repeat this stretch 3-5 times.

  2. Relaxation Techniques (3 minutes)

    • Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or mindfulness for 3 minutes to calm your mind before sleep.

Nighttime Routine (For Snoring, Bruxism, and Sleep Apnea):

  1. AveoTSD Usage (if prescribed) (Throughout the night)

    • Follow the usage instructions provided by your healthcare provider or dentist.
    • AveoTSD is typically worn during sleep to reduce snoring and sleep apnea events.

  2. MyoBrace (if prescribed) (Throughout the night)

    • Follow the usage instructions provided by your healthcare provider or dentist.
    • MyoBrace is often used at night to improve oral muscle function.

Please note that the use of AveoTSD and MyoBrace should always be prescribed and monitored by a healthcare professional. Adjust the duration and intensity of exercises and device usage based on your comfort and progress, and consult with a professional for personalized guidance if needed.
