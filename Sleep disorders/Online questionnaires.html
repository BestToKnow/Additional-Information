Here's an overview of online sleep questionnaires for varying sleep issues.

However, remember that online questionnaires cannot provide definitive diagnoses, and consulting a healthcare professional is crucial for accurate assessment and treatment.

Bruxism:

  • These questionnaires primarily focus on symptoms like teeth grinding, jaw clenching, and headaches, aiding in potential bruxism identification.

  • The Bruxism Questionnaire:

    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8051855/

  • The Oral Health Impact Profile (OHIP):

    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7648921/

Sleep Apnea:

Focusing on sleep patterns, daytime sleepiness, and symptoms like snoring, choking, or gasping during sleep, these questionnaires provide clues about potential sleep apnea.

  • STOP-BANG Questionnaire:

    https://www.mdcalc.com/calc/3992/stop-bang-score-obstructive-sleep-apnea

  • Berlin Questionnaire:

    https://www.apneaboard.com/forums/Forum-Main-Apnea-Board-Forum

Snoring:

Assessing the frequency, loudness, and impact of snoring, these questionnaires help evaluate the severity and potential need for further investigation.

  • Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS):

    https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1798888/

  • Snoring Severity Scale:

    https://www.scielo.br/j/iao/a/PG5HRQDhsWVwfbFxkXfxG8z/?format=pdf&lang=en

Insomnia:

Questions about difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, and early awakenings categorize insomnia based on their frequency and duration.

  • Insomnia Severity Index (ISI):

    https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/practice-guidelines/

  • Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI):

    https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2748771/

Important Reminder:

  • Online questionnaires offer preliminary insights but shouldn't replace professional medical evaluation.
  • Discuss your sleep concerns with your healthcare provider for diagnosis, treatment recommendations, and personalized guidance.

Additionally:

  • The accuracy of these questionnaires can vary depending on their design and validity.
  • Be truthful and complete in your answers for the most accurate results.
  • Do not solely rely on online questionnaires for managing serious sleep concerns.
